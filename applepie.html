Chris's Apple Pie

  • Home
  • Vegetarian
  • With Meat
  • Desserts

    Filling:

  • Roughly 3 granny smith apples, sliced however you'd like.
  • Granulated white sugar
  • Lemon juice
  • Flour

    • Crumble:

  • 1/2 stick of butter, unsalted
  • Brown sugar
  • Granulated white sugar
  • Salt

Filling:

Prepare pie tins and line with crust (I usually buy pre-made crust. Graham cracker crust is my favorite). Preheat oven to 400°F.

Put sliced apples in large mixing bowl. Cover with lemon juice. Add sugar and flour in the bowl with the apples in roughly a 1:3 sugar to flour ratio. Mix. Continue adding lemon, flour, and sugar until apples are evenly coated in a sticky whitish paste and no lemon juice is puddling at the bottom of the bowl.

Put apple mixture in 2 tins.

Crumble:

Melt butter in microwave safe bowl. Mix in brown and white sugar in a 1:3 brown to white ratio. Add a largish pinch of salt. Mix until there are no large clumps and crumble is dry and fairly evenly light brown. Cover apples with crumble so almost no apple is showing.

Bake until crumble is lightly toasted on top. If desired, with a clean fork, take a small bite from the side of the pan to check if the apples are fully cooked.

Makes 2 pies, roughly 16 servings. Served best warm with whipped cream and/or vanilla ice cream.
